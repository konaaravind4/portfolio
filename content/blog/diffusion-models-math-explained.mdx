# Diffusion Models Explained: The Math Behind DALL-E and Stable Diffusion

Diffusion models are the backbone of Stable Diffusion, DALL-E 3, and Imagen. Unlike GANs, they're stable to train and produce extraordinarily high-quality samples. Let's break down the mathematics.

## The Core Idea

Diffusion models learn to **reverse noise**. The forward process gradually adds Gaussian noise to an image over $T$ steps until it's pure noise. The model learns to reverse this process — denoising step by step.

## Forward Process

Given a clean image $x_0$, at each step $t$:

$$q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = \mathcal{N}(x_t; \sqrt{1-\beta_t} x_{t-1}, \beta_t \mathbf{I})$$

where $\beta_t$ is a noise schedule. Using the reparameterization trick, we can sample $x_t$ directly:

$$x_t = \sqrt{\bar{\alpha}_t} x_0 + \sqrt{1 - \bar{\alpha}_t} \epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \mathbf{I})$$

where $\bar{\alpha}_t = \prod_{s=1}^t (1 - \beta_s)$.

## Reverse Process

The model $\epsilon_\theta(x_t, t)$ is trained to predict the noise $\epsilon$ added at step $t$:

$$\mathcal{L}_{simple} = \mathbb{E}_{t, x_0, \epsilon} \left[ \| \epsilon - \epsilon_\theta(x_t, t) \|^2 \right]$$

At inference, we iteratively denoise:

$$x_{t-1} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha_t}} \left( x_t - \frac{1-\alpha_t}{\sqrt{1-\bar{\alpha}_t}} \epsilon_\theta(x_t, t) \right) + \sigma_t z$$

## Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG)

CFG allows conditioning on text without a separate classifier by jointly training conditioned and unconditioned versions:

$$\tilde{\epsilon}_\theta(x_t, c) = \epsilon_\theta(x_t, \emptyset) + \omega \cdot [\epsilon_\theta(x_t, c) - \epsilon_\theta(x_t, \emptyset)]$$

A higher guidance scale $\omega$ gives sharper, more text-aligned images at the cost of diversity.

## Why Latent Diffusion?

Running diffusion in pixel space is computationally prohibitive ($512 \times 512 \times 3$ = 786K dimensions). Latent Diffusion Models (LDM) compress images with a VAE first:

$$z_0 = \mathcal{E}(x_0), \quad x_0 \approx \mathcal{D}(z_0)$$

Diffusion runs in the 4× compressed latent space — dramatically reducing computation while preserving image quality.
